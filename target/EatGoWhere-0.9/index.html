My Story

I am a 22 year-old Malaysian currently pursuing my undergraduate at Nanyang Technological University, Singapore. I have a loving family which consists of my sister, both my parents and myself. My parents were in the education field, with my father being a lecturer at the University of Malaya back in Kuala Lumpur and my mother being a Maths teacher in one of the government schools. With that, my sister started off her career as a primary school teacher, then proceeded to further her studies with a Masters in Education at the National Institute Education, Singapore. Now, she's currently a college teacher. I wanted to be different, I didn't want to be an educator, I wanted to solve problems and help our society grow. So I chose engineering as my career path, computer engineering to be exact.

Growing up, I didn't have the academic stress that most of my friends were experiencing. With my family being in the education field, they understood that although education serves as an important base, grades are an entire separate matter that did not serve well as a measure of how educated a student really is. With that, I did not have to spend most of my free time being in tuition like most of my friends. I had free time after class and that made it important to my own personal growth. After class, I'd usually watch TV or use the computer to play my favourite games. My family enjoys watching documentaries, so that's what I grew up watching, 'How It's Made' or other animal shows on 'Animal Planet'. I am a fan of games, I am also one that likes precision, which would link up all my hobbies.

My gaming history includes famous shooter games including 'Battlefield 3' and 'Counter Strike: Global Offensive (CS:GO)', but the first game that started this passion was a game that my sister played when I was very young, 'Gunbound'. I also enjoyed games that made you grind for items, the act of doing something repeatedly for the luck of getting something good or the slow process of earning something valueable somehow made me really enjoy it. Which I still find weird. I do still game, occasionally. I still play CS:GO, PUBG and Rocket League when I have the chance to. However, I'm usually down to playing any type of games with the condition that I'm playing with friends. (except DotA 2)

Currently, my main hobby is playing snooker. When I was first introduced to snooker, snooker was like a hardcore version of pool to me. I started pool right after I graduated from my secondary school, there was a pool club nearby my house that me and my buddies would go to every few days(nights). But soon after, I quickly mastered the game of pool (or so I thought), the dopamine boost ('syiok' feeling) of scoring pool balls and winning soon vanished. So, with the objective of getting more of that 'syiokness', I started to try out snooker. But my friends didn't really enjoy it, so we sticked to pool. Then when I started out in university, there was a snooker competition that was held across the halls in my universities. I represented my hall and managed to get into the school team. Then I started to appreciate more on how skill snooker and pool players are. Remember when I said I thought I mastered pool? I was nowhere close.


SugarFree - Autonomous Maze Crawler

An Arduino based robot built to compete in a school-wide competition. The robot's role is to map out the 20x15 maze environment, find all hidden flags, get the fastest path from the starting zone to the finish zone. It was a really time-consuming project, but it was pretty fun. Written in C.
